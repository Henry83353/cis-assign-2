Legal Rights & Qualifications

Your Rights

Under the Fair Housing Act (FHA), emotional support animals are protected in no-pet housing. This means landlords must make reasonable accommodations for your ESA.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can any pet be an ESA?

Yes! While dogs and cats are most common, any domesticated animal can qualify as an ESA if it provides therapeutic benefit.

Do ESAs require special training?

No, unlike service animals, ESAs don't require specific training. Their primary role is to provide emotional comfort.

How is an ESA different from a service animal?

Service animals are trained to perform specific tasks for disabilities, while ESAs provide comfort through companionship.
